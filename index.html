[ About ]

Hello, I’m Asif, a final-year integrated Bachelor's-Master's student in Computer Science at IACS, Kolkata. I am currently working on my Master’s research under the guidance of Dr. Rajarshi Ray. I have also had the opportunity to work with Dr. Partha Basuchowdhuri at IACS.

Research Focus:

My work centers on Explainable AI (XAI) within hybrid systems. As AI systems take on more responsibility in automating complex tasks across fields like healthcare, self-driving cars, and aircraft, ensuring their safety, reliability, and interpretability becomes essential. My research aims to develop methods that guarantee these properties before the deployment of such systems. I use techniques from formal methods, automated planning, satisfiability solving, and logic to address these challenges.

Current Research:

My research focuses on understanding the unsolvability of planning tasks in hybrid systems. These tasks are modeled as planning problems, and I use search-based approaches to explore potential solutions. The analysis involves techniques such as bounded reachability and constraint relaxation, aiming to uncover the core factors that lead to unsolvability. Recently, I have started exploring causal inference to trace sequences of critical events that contribute to task failures, offering valuable insights into the behavior of complex systems.

Other Interests:

Apart from my current research, I am also interested in multi-agent systems, where autonomous agents interact using principles from algorithmic game theory. I am also keen on cyber-physical systems, which integrate computational and physical processes. My long-term goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical rigor and practical applications, contributing to the design of AI systems that are both cutting-edge and dependable.

I’m always excited to connect with others who share a passion for these topics!
